1
What is Kabaddi?
Expand

Kabaddi is a contact team sport where players send a "raider" into the opposing half to tag defenders and return without being tackled.

It blends sprint, evasion, strength and breath-control; it's played at school, amateur, and professional levels worldwide.

2
How to Play
Expand
  1. A raider crosses into the opponent's half and tries to tag defenders.
  2. The raider must chant "kabaddi" (or demonstrate continuous exhalation) to show breath control while in opponent half.
  3. If the raider returns across the midline safely, each tagged defender is out and the raider's team scores points.
  4. If the raider is tackled and cannot return, the defending team scores points.
3
Basic Rules
Expand
  • Each team typically has 7 players on court (substitutes allowed by competition rules).
  • Players must not deliberately injure opponents; fouls and misconduct are penalized.
  • The raider must stay within court lines while raiding; stepping out is a foul.
  • Holding, grabbing, or tackling must follow fair-play rules — no hair-pulling, striking, or dangerous moves.
4
Scoring
Expand

Typical scoring elements:

  • 1 point for each opponent tagged and returned.
  • Bonus point for crossing the bonus line under legal circumstances (depends on formation).
  • Super tackle extra point(s) awarded to defenders when tackling a raider with 3 or fewer defenders on court (competition-dependent).
5
Common Terms
Expand
Raider
Player who crosses into the opponent half to tag defenders.
Defender
Player(s) who try to stop the raider from returning.
Bonus Line
Line which, if legally crossed by the raider alone, can award an extra point.
Super Tackle
A situation where fewer defenders tackle the raider and an extra point is awarded.
